Mexican Lasagna Recipe

A delicious Mexican twist on traditional lasagna with layers of tortillas, seasoned ground beef, beans, and cheese!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 can (15 oz) refried beans
  • 1 can (10 oz) enchilada sauce
  • 8-10 flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chilies (optional)
  • 1 cup sour cream (for garnish)
  • Chopped cilantro (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat.
  3. Add chopped onion and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 2-3 minutes until soft.
  4. Stir in the taco seasoning, refried beans, and enchilada sauce. Simmer for 5 minutes, then remove from heat.
  5. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the beef mixture on the bottom. Top with a layer of flour tortillas (you may need to overlap them slightly).
  6. Top the tortillas with more beef mixture, then sprinkle with a mixture of cheddar and mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers until you run out of ingredients, finishing with a layer of cheese on top.
  7. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  8. Remove from the oven and let it sit for 5 minutes before serving.
  9. Garnish with sour cream and chopped cilantro. Serve hot and enjoy!

Nutritional Information

Nutrition Amount (per serving)
Calories 450 kcal
Protein 25g
Carbohydrates 40g
Fat 22g
Sodium 850mg

Serving Suggestions

Serve the Mexican Lasagna with a side of Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh salad. You can also pair it with a cold margarita or a refreshing lime-infused water.
