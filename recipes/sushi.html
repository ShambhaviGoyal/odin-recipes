Sushi

Description

Sushi is a Japanese dish of vinegared rice, typically served with vegetables, raw or cooked seafood, and sometimes egg:

Sushi rice is a medium-grained white rice prepared with vinegar and other seasonings such as salt and sugar. The usual variety of rice used for sushi is Japonica; in particular, the Koshihikari cultivar. Japonica is a medium-grained rice with somewhat rounded grains, which makes it noticeably different from the skinnier, long-grained rice that we're used to in the West, which is known as indica rice. Japonica rice is starchy, with higher levels of a starch called amylopectin than indica rice. This extra starch causes it to cook up fairly sticky, which makes it easier to eat with chopsticks, and is ideal for molding it together to make sushi

Ingredients

  • nori seaweed sheets
  • water
  • rice vinegar
  • mushrooms
  • pickled ginger
  • uncooked short-grain white rice

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F
  2. Bring water to a boil in a medium pot; stir in rice. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and water has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes
  3. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl. Gently stir into cooked rice in the pot and set aside
  4. Heat nori in the preheated oven until warm, 1 to 2 minutes
  5. Center 1 nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat. Use wet hands to spread a thin layer of rice on top. Arrange 1/4 of the crabmeat, avocado, cucumber, and pickled ginger over rice in a line down the center
