Dal Fry Recipe

A flavorful and comforting Indian dish made with spiced lentils.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow lentils (toor dal or moong dal)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water

Instructions

  1. Rinse the lentils (dal) thoroughly and cook them in a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water and a pinch of turmeric until soft. Set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the slit green chilies and chopped tomatoes. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Now add red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir and cook for a minute.
  5. Add the cooked dal (lentils) to this mixture. If needed, add more water to achieve the desired consistency. Stir well and bring it to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes to blend the flavors.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti. Enjoy!

Nutritional Information

Nutrition Amount
Calories 250 kcal (per serving)

Serving Suggestions

Dal Fry is traditionally served with steamed basmati rice or Indian flatbreads such as roti or naan. Pair with a side of vegetable curry or a fresh cucumber salad for a complete meal.
